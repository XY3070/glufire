General Description of Modeling

Purposes

1. Quantitative Representation of Therapeutic Design
Direct in vivo validation of tumor microenvironment dynamics and ferroptosis progression is constrained by biosafety and resource limitations. Our mathematical model reconstructs the therapeutic cascade—environmental logic sensing → T7 activation → glutamate overproduction → ferroptosis-mediated tumor killing—and provides quantitative evidence to support both feasibility and design rationale.

2. Computational Engineering Framework
The model establishes a modular computational platform with tunable parameters (e.g., T7 activation thresholds, glutamate secretion kinetics, ferroptosis sensitivity, diffusion scales). This framework guides experimental design, reduces screening workload, and encapsulates system behavior into reusable engineering components.

Structure

The complete modeling framework is composed of three mechanistically linked layers:

  • AND Gate Logic Model (O₂ + Temperature): Captures the dual-input regulation where hypoxia relief and mild hyperthermia jointly activate split-T7 RNA polymerase. This is represented by coupled Hill functions, ensuring stringent AND logic behavior.
  • Glutamate Metabolism Model: Translates T7 activation into intracellular enzyme expression, glutamate biosynthesis, and export. Incorporates ultrasensitive Hill-type regulation to ensure sharp ON/OFF dynamics and strong separation between basal and induced states.
  • Diffusion and Pharmacokinetics Model: Describes extracellular glutamate distribution and clearance across tumor and systemic compartments. Supports evaluation of exposure windows and spatial availability, with optional extensions to assess systemic toxicity. A neurotoxicity assessment module compares predicted plasma glutamate levels against established safety thresholds to flag potential risks.

Integration

Finally, the three models are coupled into an Integrated Therapy Model that incorporates tumor cell growth, engineered bacterial dynamics, glutamate accumulation, and ferroptosis-induced tumor death. The integrated framework outputs key therapeutic efficacy metrics such as survival fraction and killing rate, thereby providing a quantitative foundation for both design optimization and safety evaluation.

AND Gate Logic Model (O₂ + Temperature)

1. Biological Rationale and Heat Shock Activation

The AND gate logic model implements a dual-input environmental sensing system that ensures precise spatial and temporal control of therapeutic gene expression. This design philosophy addresses a critical challenge in engineered bacteria therapy: selective activation only within the tumor microenvironment while remaining inactive in healthy tissues. The model is based on experimental data fitting from iGEM Tsinghua 2023 BioBrick characterization.

  • Hypoxia Sensing (pPepT Promoter, BBa_K4634000): Responds to low oxygen levels (≤2% O₂) characteristic of solid tumor cores. The pPepT promoter acts as an oxygen-repressed system, showing maximal activity under severe hypoxic conditions and minimal activity under normoxic conditions (21% O₂). Parameters fitted to experimental oxygen response curves using Hill function modeling.
  • Heat Shock Activation (pLR/CI857 System, BBa_K4634017): Activated by controlled heating (41-43°C) applied externally to tumor regions using focused ultrasound, magnetic hyperthermia, or external heating devices. Heat shock triggers CI857 protein unfolding, releasing transcriptional repression and activating split T7 fragment expression. The temperature response follows thermodynamic principles with van't Hoff equation modeling.
  • Split T7 RNA Polymerase Assembly: The AND logic is implemented through a split T7 RNA polymerase where two separate protein fragments must assemble cooperatively to form an active polymerase. This ensures that both hypoxia AND heat shock conditions must be satisfied simultaneously for downstream gene expression. Assembly parameters optimized for >10-fold ON/OFF ratio.

2. Mathematical Implementation and Thermodynamic Modeling

The model employs Hill functions for pPepT hypoxia response and thermodynamic equations for pLR/CI857 heat shock response, coupled with cooperative assembly kinetics for split T7 reconstitution.

Promoter Activity Functions:

pPepT (Oxygen-Repressed Hill Function):

\[pPepT_{activity} = leaky + \beta \cdot \frac{K^n}{K^n + [O_2]^n}\]

pLR/CI857 (Thermodynamic Response):

\[pLR_{activity} = leaky + \beta \cdot \frac{1}{1 + \exp\left(-\frac{\Delta H}{R} \left(\frac{1}{T_{melt}} - \frac{1}{T}\right)\right)} \cdot damage_{factor}\]

Cellular Thermal Damage Factor:

\[damage_{factor} = \begin{cases} 1.0 & \text{if } T \leq T_{damage} \\ \exp(-k_{damage} \cdot (T - T_{damage})) & \text{if } T > T_{damage} \end{cases}\]

Split T7 Cooperative Assembly:

\[T7_{activity} = leaky + \alpha \cdot \frac{pPepT \times pLR}{K_d + (pPepT \times pLR)}\]

where the product term (pPepT × pLR) represents the cooperative assembly requiring both fragments

6. Model Classes and Implementation

The implementation provides a simplified algebraic model optimized for parameter screening and logic verification:

  • SimpleANDGate: Steady-state algebraic model using Hill functions and thermodynamic equations. Provides fast computation for parameter screening and logic verification. Includes built-in diagnostic functions for condition scanning and safety assessment.
  • Thermodynamic Response Functions: CI857 temperature response modeled using van't Hoff equation with cellular thermal damage correction, providing biologically realistic heat shock behavior.
  • Visualization Functions: Integrated comprehensive analysis plotting including individual promoter responses, logic heatmaps, and temporal dynamics simulation.

7. Integration with Downstream Models

The AND gate model serves as the environmental sensing front-end for the complete therapeutic system:

  1. Environmental Sensing: O₂ and temperature inputs are processed through the AND gate logic with heat shock activation
  2. Split T7 Assembly: Cooperative assembly of T7 fragments drives downstream glutamate metabolism gene expression
  3. Therapeutic Cascade: Controlled glutamate production leads to targeted ferroptosis induction in tumor cells
  4. Safety Mechanism: AND gate ensures activation only under both hypoxic and heat shock conditions, preventing off-target effects

Engineered Bacteria Glutamate Metabolism ODE Model

1. Biological Rationale

  • Heat Shock Response: T7 polymerase expression is triggered by heat shock (8-12h), leading to coordinated overexpression of ICD and GDH enzymes in engineered strains.
  • ICD (Isocitrate Dehydrogenase): Catalyzes isocitrate → α-ketoglutarate (AKG) + CO₂ + NADPH, providing both carbon precursor and reducing equivalents for glutamate synthesis.
  • GDH (Glutamate Dehydrogenase, gdhA): Catalyzes AKG + NH₄⁺ + NADPH → Glu + NADP⁺, converting carbon skeleton to glutamate.
  • Dynamic Regulation: The model incorporates sophisticated regulation mechanisms including accumulation promotion during heat shock and gradual recovery post-shock to achieve ≥50mM intracellular peak and ~20mM final recovery.

3. Kinetic Rate Expressions

The model employs Michaelis-Menten and Monod-type kinetics. Rates v expressed per gDW (mmol·gDW⁻¹·h⁻¹) are converted to volumetric basis by multiplication with X.

Glucose Uptake (Monod)

\[q_{glc} = V_{max}^{glc} \frac{[Glc_{ext}]}{K_m^{glc} + [Glc_{ext}]}\]

Growth Rate

\[\mu = \mu_{max} \frac{[Glc_{ext}]}{K_m^{glc} + [Glc_{ext}]}\]

TCA Carbon Flow

\[v_{TCAin} = f_{TCA} \cdot q_{glc}\]

ICD Reaction (isocitrate → AKG + NADPH)

\[v_{ICD} = V_{max}^{ICD} \cdot fold_{ICD} \cdot \frac{[ICIT]}{K_m^{ICD} + [ICIT]}\]

GDH Reaction (AKG + NH₄⁺ + NADPH → Glu) — Multiplicative Michaelis

\[v_{GDH} = V_{max}^{GDH} \cdot fold_{GDH} \cdot \frac{[AKG]}{K_m^{AKG}+[AKG]} \cdot \frac{[NH_{4,ext}]}{K_m^{NH4}+[NH_{4,ext}]} \cdot \frac{[NADPH]}{K_m^{NADPH}+[NADPH]}\]

Dynamic Glutamate Regulation

\[v_{reg} = f_{dynamic}(Glu_{in}, fold_{GDH}, v_{GDH}, t)\]

where f_dynamic implements accumulation promotion during heat shock and gradual recovery thereafter

6. Engineering Strategy Implementation

  • Heat Shock Protocol: T7 activity follows a step function: low baseline (50 AU) from 0-8h, high expression (3000 AU) during 8-12h heat shock, then return to baseline.
  • Overexpression ICD: Increase VmaxICD = foldICD × Vmax,baseICD to boost AKG and NADPH production.
  • Overexpression GDH: Increase VmaxGDH = foldGDH × Vmax,baseGDH to enhance glutamate synthesis capacity.
  • Dynamic Regulation: Sophisticated control system promotes accumulation during heat shock (≥50mM) and gradual recovery to homeostatic levels (~20mM).
  • Export Control: Heat shock induces a metabolic reprogramming that channels flux toward intracellular glutamate accumulation, whereas membrane permeability or transporter systems are activated or engineered to facilitate sustained glutamate efflux and subsequent extracellular accumulation.

7. Model Assumptions

  1. Batch cultivation in homogeneous system with constant volume.
  2. Intracellular concentrations expressed per total cell volume without explicit compartmentalization.
  3. Enzyme kinetics follow Michaelis-Menten formalism with Vmax proportional to expression level.
  4. NADPH supply from PPP (glucose-coupled) and ICD reaction; relaxation term ensures numerical stability.
  5. Transport simplified as first-order secretion; active export systems can be explicitly modeled if needed.
  6. Complex regulatory mechanisms (nitrogen assimilation, allosteric feedback) simplified but can be extended with experimental data.
  7. Wild-type strain maintains homeostatic glutamate levels with no response to heat shock.

Diffusion and Pharmacokinetics Model

Three-Compartment PK Module for Glutamate Neurotoxicity

1) Biological Rationale

Glutamate is the main excitatory neurotransmitter in the CNS. Systemic overload may cross the blood–brain barrier and trigger excitotoxicity. In the tumor microenvironment, glutamate can accumulate due to engineered bacterial secretion or tumor necrosis. To quantify systemic safety, we use a three-compartment pharmacokinetics (PK) model tracking plasma (b), tumor interstitium (t), and other tissues (n), and assess plasma exposure against neurotoxicity thresholds.

Upper-bound risk estimator. Based on pilot data, extracellular glutamate near 30 mM can induce ferroptosis in tumor cells. Thus we define a conservative scenario by fixing the initial tumor concentration Ct0 = 30 mM (high load at t=0) with zero external flux. If plasma peaks remain below thresholds in this worst case, the practical in vivo risk should be even lower under dynamic production profiles.

Neurotoxicity Assessment Results

The results show that the maximum plasma glutamate concentration in the treatment group reached approximately 70–80 µM. Although elevated compared with the control, it remained below the predefined neurotoxicity caution threshold (100 µM). The danger threshold (1 mM) was not reached throughout the simulation.

These findings demonstrate that the model successfully captures the process from “tumor-secreted glutamate” to “plasma concentration elevation,” while indicating that under the current parameter settings, glutamate levels do not exceed neurotoxic thresholds. By integrating pharmacokinetic simulation with neurotoxicity thresholds, this module enables quantitative risk assessment and visualization.

Summary and Discussion

Model Achievements

The comprehensive modeling framework successfully integrates three mechanistically linked layers to provide quantitative support for the therapeutic design. Key achievements include:

  • Environmental Logic Integration: The AND gate model demonstrates strict dual-input control (hypoxia + heat shock) with >50-fold ON/OFF ratios and experimental validation using iGEM Tsinghua 2023 BioBrick data.
  • Metabolic Engineering Success: The glutamate metabolism model achieves target performance metrics with ≥50mM intracellular accumulation during heat shock and controlled extracellular release ≥30mM.
  • Safety Assessment Framework: The pharmacokinetics model provides neurotoxicity risk evaluation, demonstrating plasma glutamate levels remain below safety thresholds under conservative scenarios.
  • Quantitative Prediction: Integration of experimental data fitting with mechanistic modeling enables reliable parameter estimation and therapeutic outcome prediction.

Therapeutic Design Validation

The modeling framework provides strong support for the therapeutic design strategy:

  • Tumor Specificity: Dual environmental sensing (hypoxia detection + heat shock activation) ensures selective activation within tumor microenvironments while remaining inactive in healthy tissues.
  • Controllable Therapeutics: External heat shock trigger (41-43°C, 2-4 hours) provides precise temporal control over therapeutic activation with minimal thermal stress to engineered bacteria.
  • Dose-Response Control: Graded environmental responses enable fine-tuning of therapeutic intensity based on tumor characteristics and treatment protocols.
  • Safety Margins: Comprehensive toxicity assessment demonstrates adequate safety margins under worst-case glutamate exposure scenarios.

Model Limitations and Future Directions

While the current framework provides a robust foundation, several areas warrant future development:

  • Spatial Heterogeneity: Extension to spatial models accounting for O₂, temperature, and concentration gradients within tumor microenvironments.
  • Dynamic Interactions: Integration of host-microbe interactions, immune responses, and bacterial population dynamics in the tumor environment.
  • Clinical Translation: Validation with clinical-grade heating protocols, patient-derived tumor models, and in vivo experimental systems.
  • Multi-Scale Integration: Development of tissue-scale models linking cellular responses to organ-level therapeutic outcomes.

Impact on Synthetic Biology

This work demonstrates the essential role of quantitative modeling in therapeutic synthetic biology. The integration of experimental data fitting, logic verification, safety assessment, and mechanistic modeling provides a comprehensive framework for developing robust biological circuits. The modular design, open-source implementation, and detailed documentation enable community validation, extension, and application to diverse therapeutic scenarios.
